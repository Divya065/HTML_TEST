THE INDIAN CRICKETER VIRAT KOHLI

Virat Kohli, born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, is one of the most celebrated cricketers of his generation. Renowned for his aggressive batting style and remarkable consistency, Kohli made his debut for the Indian national team in 2008. Over the years, he has shattered numerous records, becoming one of the fastest players to score 8,000, 9,000, and 10,000 runs in One Day Internationals (ODIs). His ability to chase down targets has earned him the reputation of being one of the best chasers in the game, making him a key player in high-pressure situations.

Beyond his on-field exploits, Kohli is also known for his leadership qualities. He captained the Indian team across all formats, leading them to significant victories and helping to foster a strong team culture. Off the field, Kohli is recognized for his fitness advocacy and philanthropic efforts, supporting various causes, including child health and education. His passion for the game, combined with his commitment to fitness and social issues, has made him an influential figure not just in cricket, but in Indian sports as a whole.
